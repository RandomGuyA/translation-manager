Visit the Brecon Beacons National Park Visitor Centre and Mynydd Illtyd

The National Park Visitor Centre or  €˜Mountain Centre' is located
7km south west of Brecon and is one of the most popular visitor
attractions in the Brecon Beacons National Park. The Centre
stands 1100ft (330m) above sea level and offers stunning views
of the highest mountains (Pen y Fan and Corn Du) in SouthWales.

In the Centre there are interpretation boards, maps and short  films which explain the history of the National Park and the Brecon  Beacons. The three dimensional relief map of the entire National  Park is an extremely popular exhibit and staff are on hand to  assist with any enquiries you may have about the National Park.

Throughout the year there are events available such as guided  walks and children's activities, copies of which can be found at the  Centre and on the Guided Walks, Children's Activities and Family  Events pages on our website www.visitbreconbeacons.com/events. Other  facilities include the popular tearooms serving local produce and  home cooked food, a gift shop and easy access toilet facilities.  Outside, families and children can enjoy the large grassed area in  front of the building for picnics or to play games.

The adjacent common land, Mynydd Illtyd, offers plenty of easy walks  with fantastic views. Route maps are available in the shop or go to  the Wildlife Walks page on the website and follow the link to Mynydd  Illtyd for a nature walk. For mountain biking there are two marked  routes to choose from; the easy family Visitor Centre Route or the  more challenging Mynydd Illtyd route, both of which can be found  on our mountain biking pages at www.mtbbreconbeacons.co.uk.
Bikes can be hired locally (Brecon) and can even be delivered to  the Centre  €“ see the Guides and Hire pages.
